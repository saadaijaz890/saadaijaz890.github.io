NIGHT ANXIETY

How to Stop an Old Dog From Crying at Night

Senior dog anxiety is a different beast — often rooted in cognitive decline, pain, or disorientation. Here's the exact nighttime routine that brings lasting peace.

🔬 Vet-reviewed📅 Updated 2026⏱ 8 min read

Why Senior Dogs Cry at Night

Nighttime crying in dogs over 7 years old is rarely "just anxiety." Here are the most common root causes — identifying the right one changes which solution works:

🧠

Canine Cognitive Dysfunction (CCD)

Dog dementia affects up to 68% of dogs aged 15–16. Symptoms mirror Alzheimer's: disorientation, reversed sleep cycles, nighttime waking. This is the most common cause in dogs 10+.

🦴

Pain or Arthritis

Lying still at night intensifies joint pain. A dog who paces or can't settle is often uncomfortable, not anxious. See your vet to rule this out first.

👀

Vision or Hearing Loss

Darkness is terrifying for a dog losing sight. They can't orient themselves and panic. A night light alone can dramatically reduce crying.

😰

Generalized Anxiety

Some dogs simply become more anxious with age as their nervous system changes. Products and routine are highly effective here.

The Senior Dog Nighttime Routine

🌙 Evening Protocol — Start This Tonight

6:00 PM

Final walk or gentle exercise — tires them out without overstimulating. Keep it calm and slow for arthritic dogs.

7:30 PM

Give Zylkene or NaturVet Quiet Moments chew with dinner. These need 1–2 hours to reach full effect.

9:00 PM

Plug in Adaptil diffuser near sleeping area. Spray a small amount on their bedding. Dim the lights — start their circadian wind-down.

9:30 PM

Leave a small night light on if vision decline is suspected. Place a worn t-shirt of yours near their bed.

Bedtime

Place them on their orthopedic bed (joint pain matters). White noise machine on. Do NOT give long emotional goodbyes — calm, brief, then leave.

Melatonin Dosing (always consult your vet first): General guideline is 0.1mg/kg, 30 minutes before bedtime. Do NOT use in dogs with hormonal conditions (Cushing's, diabetes) or intact females.

When to See Your Vet

If crying persists beyond 2 weeks of consistent protocol, schedule a vet visit. Cognitive dysfunction syndrome can be managed with Anipryl (selegiline), and pain-related nighttime anxiety is treated entirely differently. Early intervention makes a huge difference in quality of life for senior dogs.
