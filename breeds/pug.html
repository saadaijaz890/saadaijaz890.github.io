Why Pugs Are So Prone to Anxiety

Pugs were bred for centuries as companion dogs — their entire evolutionary purpose is human companionship. This makes them exceptionally loving, but also exceptionally prone to separation anxiety and general stress. They're also brachycephalic (flat-faced), meaning stress physically impacts their breathing more than other breeds.

Pug-Specific Warning: Stress can trigger respiratory distress in Pugs faster than in other breeds. If your Pug is panting heavily, making honking sounds, or has blue-tinged gums during an anxiety episode — this is a medical emergency. Call your vet immediately.
