How Long Can You Leave an Australian Shepherd Alone? (2026 Guide)

Australian Shepherds are known for their intelligence and energy, making them both a joy and a challenge to own. Understanding how long you can leave an Australian Shepherd alone is crucial to prevent anxiety and ensure their happiness.

🔬 Vet-reviewed📅 Updated 2026⏱ 7 min read

The Need for Company

Australian Shepherds thrive on interaction and activity. As a breed with very high multi-type anxiety risk, these dogs do not do well when left alone for extended periods. Ideally, an Australian Shepherd should not be left alone for more than 4-6 hours at a time. Their active nature and intelligent minds mean they need both physical and mental stimulation throughout the day.

Leaving an Australian Shepherd alone for too long can lead to anxiety, manifesting in behaviors such as excessive barking, destructive habits, and attempts to escape. These behaviors are often signs of distress due to separation anxiety, a common issue in this breed.

Exercise: A Key Component

One of the most effective ways to mitigate anxiety in Australian Shepherds is through ample exercise. With a minimum daily exercise requirement of over two hours, these dogs need a variety of activities to keep them satisfied. A combination of walks, playtime, and agility training can help burn off excess energy and reduce anxiety levels.

Ensuring your Australian Shepherd is well-exercised before you leave can greatly reduce their anxiety. A tired dog is more likely to relax and nap when alone, rather than becoming anxious and restless.

Interactive Toys and Mental Stimulation

Given their high intelligence, Australian Shepherds require more than just physical exercise. Providing them with interactive toys and puzzles can help engage their minds and keep them occupied while you're away. These tools can prevent boredom, which is another major trigger for anxiety in Australian Shepherds.

Consider toys that dispense treats or those that require problem-solving skills, as these can provide essential mental stimulation. For instance, puzzle toys can be a great way to keep your Australian Shepherd engaged and happy.

Calming Aids to Consider

For Australian Shepherds prone to anxiety, calming aids can be an invaluable resource. Products like anxiety wraps or calming diffusers can help soothe stressed dogs. These aids work by providing a sense of security or releasing soothing pheromones into the environment.

It's essential to choose the right size and type of product for your Australian Shepherd to ensure their comfort and effectiveness. Always monitor your pet's response to these products and consult with a veterinarian if you have concerns.

Pro Tip: Establishing a consistent daily routine can help your Australian Shepherd feel more secure and less anxious when left alone.
Warning: Never leave an Australian Shepherd alone for an entire day. Extended isolation can significantly exacerbate anxiety-related behaviors.

Frequently Asked Questions: Australian Shepherd Alone Time

Can Australian Shepherds be left alone during the day?
Australian Shepherds are best left alone for no more than 4-6 hours. Their high energy levels and need for mental stimulation mean they require frequent interaction.
What are the signs of anxiety in an Australian Shepherd?
Common signs include excessive barking, destructive behavior, and attempts to escape. Separation anxiety is a frequent issue in this breed.
How can I reduce anxiety in my Australian Shepherd when I'm away?
Providing interactive toys and ensuring they have plenty of exercise can help. Consider using calming aids such as diffusers or anxiety wraps.
